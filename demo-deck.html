The problem was never information.
It's that trying, every day, wears you down.
Adherence to long-term therapies averages ~50% — WHO (Sabaté), 2003.
People keep going when they feel supported —
not when they're pushed.
Autonomy-supportive care predicts better health outcomes than controlling care — Ng et al., Perspect. Psychol. Sci., 2012 (self-determination theory, meta-analysis).
Feel Good is an AI companion for living with a long illness. Rather than tell you what to do, it notices the good you did, nudges you gently, and stays with you when it's hard.
First, it just learns you.
The day — through a wearable camera
↓  on device: video → events, then the video is deleted
A stream of daily eventsate at 1 · took the stairs · meds · sat 3 hrs
↓  week one: no output — it just learns your normal
Your baseline — your own normal rhythm
Then it does three things — all from your own data.
Morningreads your patterns → one easy swap, within your taste, never “don't”“3:30 slump — coffee's a 4-min walk”
Eveningreads today's events → keeps the good you did (≤3), never the bad“stairs · cooked · walked home”
Real-timelive signal vs your baseline → fires only when you're clearly stuck“want to talk it through?” — no advice
Suggestions and reflections only — it never issues a command.
Feedback & self-monitoring: Michie et al., 2013. Brief, non-directive support beats advice-giving: Rubak et al., 2005.
Every other app adds pressure.
Ours takes it away.
You don't push people harder.
You make the long haul liveable.
