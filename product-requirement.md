
## 1. What the product is (context you need)

An AI wellness companion for someone who **wants to improve their everyday habits** (self-motivated
adult; obesity / type-2 diabetes / cardiovascular risk are the backdrop, but this is a consumer
product, not a medical device).

The user wears **discreet glasses** that passively record their day (a "life-vlog"). Footage stays in
**their own cloud** — privacy is theirs. From what the glasses saw, the product produces **exactly two
small cards per day** and is otherwise silent:

- **Morning card** — one gentle suggestion, based on the user's *past patterns*. Pre-shapes the day's
  choices. Never a command.
- **Evening card** — honest praise that surfaces **good choices the user didn't notice they were
  making** (took the stairs, cooked instead of ordering in, walked the long way home).

**Guiding principle: "feel good — change happily."** Smallest possible nudge, always inside what the
user already likes, never punitive.

### Hard rules (these are the product's soul — do not violate)
1. **Never fabricate.** Every praised item must come from a real event in the day's data. No invented
   step counts, no rounding up. The product's whole value is being an *honest mirror*.
2. **Never say "don't."** Suggestions are only ever positive, additive, or lateral (a healthier version
   of the same thing, a slightly shorter portion, the walkable option among options the user already
   wanted).
3. **Silence is the default.** Two cards a day. No notification spam.
4. **Stay inside the user's tastes.** If they like a flavour of crisps, suggest the same flavour, just a
   better/smaller version. Learn from their responses.

---

## 2. Explicit NON-goals (do not build these — they contradict the product)
- ❌ No biomarker dashboards, no charts of weight/glucose/BP over time. The product is about
  **lifestyle**, not biometrics.
- ❌ No calorie counting or any number that invites self-judgement.
- ❌ No streaks / guilt mechanics. Breaking a streak feels bad — banned.
- ❌ No medical intake forms, no diagnoses, no "consult your doctor" clinical framing.
- ❌ No login/signup, no real accounts, no analytics.

Keep it tiny. If a feature isn't in Section 5, don't add it.
